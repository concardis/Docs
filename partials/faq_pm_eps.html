Overview


EPS is an Internet payment System in Austria, based on online banking. This payment method allows customers to buy securely on the Internet using direct online transfers from their bank account.


EPS offers merchants a real-time payment method (publicized as virtually risk-free) to accept internet payments. For customers, EPS uses the same environment as their banks' online banking sites. The level of security depends on the participating bank. Some Austrian Banks offer two-factor authentication (2FA), such as a challenge-response access token based on the chip embedded in the debit card or ATM card. Others, however, offer simpler PIN and TAN based online banking services. No sensitive information is being shared with the merchant, such as credit card or Giro account numbers.


Customer (buyer)


For the payment with EPS, customers only need an online banking checking account with a bank or savings bank participating in EPS. The authentication takes place via PIN and TAN. Online payments are 100% guaranteed from the buyer's bank for amounts up to 5000 euros.


For customers EPS works as follows:

  1. Merchant offers EPS as payment method, often in addition to the regular credit card payment options
  2. Consumer selects EPS and selects their bank
  3. Consumer is redirected to their bank's login page
  4. Participating bank displays transaction data
  5. Customer enters account number, PIN, and either:
    1. A remittance slip is sent to the customer for confirming the transaction, containing a TAN (transaction number). The customer enters this number to confirm the transaction.
    2. The customer signs the transaction digitally using a 2FA token (if their bank offers that service)
  6. Bank authorizes transaction in real-time, deducting the amount directly from the consumer's account (if there is not enough balance, the transaction will be refused)
  7. Merchant received real-time confirmation of the payment by the bank
  8. Consumer is redirected back to the merchant page with a confirmation that the payment has been successful

Merchant (dealer)


Dealer receives a payment guarantee from the buyer's bank immediately after successful EPS transfer and can thus issue the goods immediately and without risk.


EPSs provide you with a payment procedure with dealer protection. Thanks to the payment guarantee from the buyer's bank, payment defaults and refunds are not possible by the buyer. The money will be credited to your bank account one banking day after the transaction.


Security and privacy EPS is part of the online banking of the dividing banks and savings banks. Therefore, the high security and privacy standards of the Austrian banking industry apply.

Technical Flow


Step 1: Set up EPS Bank accounts


Before a EPS transaction can be created, you must setup your EPS Bank account in Merchant Center.

Set up the EPS bank account:

  • Go to "Payment methods" section in Merchant Center and select "EPS" Payment method

  • It will open new page to fill bank account data for EPS. This is the account where the merchant will received the money. Fill the data and save the page.

  • Contact Account administrator to enable EPS payments.

Step 2: Create debit


Once the Buyer chooses EPS payment, he must enter his bank information (BIC) in order to be able to check whether the bank supports EPS payments. After that he will be transfered to his online banking to proceed with the payment. His Bank will authorise the transaction in real-time, and if there is not enough balance, the transaction will be refused. The debit causes the immediately charging of the full amount.


Step 3: Confirm that the charge has succeeded


For EPS payments the funds are not immediately available. The state of the EPS payment is almost immediately changed from "PENDING" to "OK", but the funds will appear in the merchant account within a few days (usually the next day)

Specifics


EPS Bank List request


When the integrator wants to add EPS as available payment option, he must first call /bankDetails endpoint to get the current bank list for EPS.


Before every new payment the merchant will call get bankDetails and will use the response for showing the updated list with available banks.


EPS Payment specific request parameters


PARAMETER MANDATORY/OPTIONAL DESCRIPTION EXAMPLE
payment.iban M IBAN of the customer DE15111111111111111199
payment.bankName M name of the customer bank
payment.bic M BIC of the customer bank account BENEDEPPY
payment.accountHolder M Name of the bank account holder Max Mustermann
payment.successUrl M Redirect URL for successful payment http://my.shop.com/success
payment.failureUrl M Redirect URL for unsuccessful payment http://my.shop.com/fail
payment.cancelUrl M Redirect URL when payment is canceled http://my.shop.com/cancel
