Overview


Giropay is an Internet payment System in Germany, based on online banking. This payment method allows customers to buy securely on the Internet using direct online transfers from their bank account.


Giropay offers merchants a real-time payment method (publicized as virtually risk-free) to accept internet payments. For customers, Giropay uses the same environment as their banks' online banking sites. The level of security depends on the participating bank. Some German Banks offer two-factor authentication (2FA), such as a challenge-response access token based on the chip embedded in the debit card or ATM card. Others, however, offer simpler PIN and TAN based online banking services. No sensitive information is being shared with the merchant, such as credit card or Giro account numbers.


Customer (buyer)


For the payment with Giropay, customers only need an online banking checking account with a bank or savings bank participating in Giropay. The authentication takes place via PIN and TAN. Online payments are 100% guaranteed from the buyer's bank for amounts up to 5000 euros.


For customers Giropay works as follows:

  1. Merchant offers Giropay as payment method, often in addition to the regular credit card payment options
  2. Consumer selects Giropay and selects their bank
  3. Consumer is redirected to their bank's login page
  4. Participating bank displays transaction data
  5. Customer enters account number, PIN, and either:
    1. A remittance slip is sent to the customer for confirming the transaction, containing a TAN (transaction number). The customer enters this number to confirm the transaction.
    2. The customer signs the transaction digitally using a 2FA token (if their bank offers that service)
  6. Bank authorizes transaction in real-time, deducting the amount directly from the consumer's account (if there is not enough balance, the transaction will be refused)
  7. Merchant received real-time confirmation of the payment by the bank
  8. Consumer is redirected back to the merchant page with a confirmation that the payment has been successful

Merchant


Dealer receives a payment guarantee from the buyer's bank immediately after successful Giropay transfer and can thus issue the goods immediately and without risk.


Giropays provide you with a payment procedure with merchant protection. Thanks to the payment guarantee from the buyer's bank, payment defaults and refunds are not possible by the buyer. The money will be credited to your bank account one banking day after the transaction.

Technical Flow


Step 1: Set up Giropay Bank accounts


Before a Giropay transaction can be created, you must setup your Giropay Bank account in Merchant Center.

Set up the Giropay bank account:

Go to "Payment methods" section in Merchant Center and select "Giropay" Payment method

Test merchant bank account:

Owner: Max Muster

IBAN: DE44100500001654698497

BIC: BELADEBEXXX

  • Go to "Payment methods" section in Merchant Center and select "Giropay" Payment method

  • It will open new page to fill bank account data for Giropay. This is the account where the merchant will received the money. Fill the data and save the page.

  • Contact Account administrator to enable Giropay payments.

Step 2: Create debit


Once the Buyer chooses Giropay payment, he must enter his bank information (BIC) in order to be able to check whether the bank supports Giropay payments. After that he will be transfered to his online banking to proceed with the payment. His Bank will authorise the transaction in real-time, and if there is not enough balance, the transaction will be refused. The debit causes the immediately charging of the full amount.


Step 3: Confirm that the charge has succeeded


For Giropay payments the funds are not immediately available. The state of the Giropay payment is almost immediately changed from "PENDING" to "OK", but the funds will appear in the merchant account within a few days (usually the next day)


Please notify:

Your integration needs to use notifications to be notified of status changes on debit

The Giropay notifications could be subscribed

  • via the payengine API (Link to API documentation)
  • via the Payengine merchant center (Link to Merchant Center notifications documentation)

The following events are sent when the charge’s status is updated:

EVENT DESCRIPTION
debit.success The charge succeeded and the payment is complete.

Specifics


Giropay Bank List request


When the integrator wants to add Giropay as available payment option, he must first call /bankDetails endpoint to get the current bank list for Giropay.


Before every new payment the merchant will call get bankDetails and will use the response for showing the updated list with available banks.


Giropay Payment specific request parameters


PARAMETER MANDATORY/OPTIONAL DESCRIPTION EXAMPLE
payment.iban M IBAN of the customer DE15111111111111111199
payment.bankName M name of the customer bank
payment.bic M BIC of the customer bank account BENEDEPPY
payment.accountHolder O Name of the bank account holder Max Mustermann
payment.successUrl M Redirect URL for successful payment http://my.shop.com/success
payment.failureUrl M Redirect URL for unsuccessful payment http://my.shop.com/fail
payment.cancelUrl M Redirect URL when payment is canceled http://my.shop.com/cancel
