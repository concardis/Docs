Overview


Klarna is one of Europe's largest banks, providing easy to use payment solutions and working seamlessly across borders. Klarna offers direct payments, pay after delivery options and installment plans in a smooth and safe purchase experience that lets consumers pay in a simple way when and how they prefer to. Klarna assumes both credit and fraud risk for merchants and consumers throughout the full customer journey.

  • Pay Now - customers pay in full at checkout
  • Pay Later - customers buy now, but pay in full after certain amount of time
  • Slice It - customers buy now, but pay later in fixed or flexible slices

Merchants can choose which of these payment methods should be enabled for them, depending on their preferences and specific characteristics of their business.


Customer Experience

  1. After the customer is done with the web checkout and triggers the payment process, he/she will see a dialog with available payment methods.

  2. After the customer selects Klarna all available Klarna payment methods (enabled by the merchant) will be displayed.

  3. When the customer selects one of the options he/she will be redirected to the Hosted Payment Page of Klarna.

    For Pay Later method, Hosted Payment Page of Klarna will present a screen with the following structure:

  4. After customer clicks on Buy button, a dialog window for collecting additional data will be displayed.

  5. After customer fills required date of birth and clicks on Continue button, he/she will be redirected to either Success or Failure page.

Step 2 A: Preauth & Capture


Mandatory Object

'Basket' is a mandatory object for all transaction requests of all three Klarna payment methods.


The structure of 'Basket' object must be consistent for all transaction requests related to a given order.


The order of basket items in the Authorizing transaction (Preauth or Debit) requests defines the order of basket items for all subsequent transaction (Capture, Cancel and Refund) requests.


Preauth & Capture

The 'Preauth' transaction unlike the 'Debit' transaction isn't directly booking the initial amount of the order, it should be followed by 'Capture' transaction in order to receive the funds.

Cancel


Any pre-authorized order can still be cancelled as long as the order has not been captured.

A 'Cancel' transaction can be created for amount not bigger than initial amount of 'Preauth' transaction.

Klarna payment methods enable full and partial cancelations of the initial amount of the order.

Notifications


Notifications for merchants working with Klarna payment methods can be set up in two ways:


  • via API - with scope only transaction events on order level. More details can be found under the following link -Notifications
  • via Merchant Center - with scope full rage of possible events. More details can be found under the following link https://docs.payengine.de/merchantcenter/managenotification


Testing


Testing of the Klarna payment methods can be executed directly via Klarna Sandbox.


The Klarna Sandbox is a self-contained virtual testing environment that mimics the live Klarna production environment.


Test credentials for Klarna Sandbox can be received from Klarna.
