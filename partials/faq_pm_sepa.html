GENERAL


What is SEPA?

 

Single Euro Payments Area (SEPA) is an initiative of the European Payment Council (EPC) which aims at easing the process of payments within the Europe. Please visit EPC website section About SEPA (https://www.europeanpaymentscouncil.eu/about-sepa) for further information.


What is the payment method flow?


The SEPA Direct Debit in Payengine follows an asynchronous flow in which the confirmation of processing the payment is received only after a delay. Until the confirmation is received the transaction status is OK meaning that the Payengine accepts the request but further processing is still pending. After processing the payment the status of the transaction will change to either success or failure depending on the result.


With Payengine’s SEPA Deirect Debit your customer won’t be redirected out of your shop integration and after providing his payment details no further action is required on his side.


Optionally the Payengine provides a convenient way to setup email prenotifications for SEPA Direct Debit. There is a general prenotification template which can be found below. For prenotifications you should enable them via the Merchant Center and also provide customer email within the order request.


More about prenotifications...

Pre-notification ("Pre-Notification") is any notification (eg invoice, policy, contract) of the direct debit recipient to the payer, which announces a debit via SEPA direct debit. The preliminary information must contain the due date and the exact amount and can also announce several direct debits. It must be sent to the payer in due time (at least 14 calendar days before the due date, unless a different period has been agreed with the payer) before the due date, so that he can adjust to the account balance and provide adequate cover. The manner in which the preliminary information can be given results from the regulations of the respective debt collection agreement between the payee and his payment service provider.

What the customer needs to provide for SEPA Direct Debit?


The customer should provide a valid IBAN, BIC, Account Holder, Bank Name name.

He needs as well to agree with a Mandate statement which should be displayed to him on the payment form.

Based on this information a Payengine Payment Instrument is created securely which can be used for either onetime or recurring payments.


What is a Mandate?


The SEPA mandate is usually a paper document, but since it is not possible to implement a paper document in eCommerce, electronic mandates (eMandates) are used. The Mandates are stored within the Payengine Payment Instrument.


These mandates have a unique identifier and store the text (see the text below) and timestamp the customer agreed to this specific mandate.


SEPA Mandatstext

Ich ermächtige die Concardis GmbH, Helfman-Park 7 in 65760 Eschborn, GläubigerID DE82CC000000346626, Zahlungen von meinem Konto mittels SEPA-Lastschrift unter der Mandatsreferenz [mandateId] einzuziehen. Zugleich weise ich mein Kreditinstitut an, die von Concardis GmbH auf mein Konto gezogenen SEPA-Lastschriften einzulösen.
Hinweis: Ich kann innerhalb von acht Wochen, beginnend mit dem Belastungsdatum, die Erstattung des belasteten Betrages verlangen. Es gelten dabei die mit meinem Kreditinstitut vereinbarten Bedingungen.

Verkürzung der Ankündigungsfrist:
Die Frist für die Vorabinformation der SEPA-Lastschrift wird auf einen Tag verkürzt.


Apart from that within Payengine the mandate object of a payment instrument is used for tracking the usage of a payment instrument through the type property.


There are four types of direct debit mandate:


Mandate directDebitType Description Purpose
Single One execution Onetime payments
First The initial usage of the payment instrument Recurring Payments
Recurring Any further usage of the payment instrument Recurring Payments
Last The last usage of a payment instrument Recurring Payments

RECURRING PAYMENTS


What are recurring payments?


When a customer authorizes you to perform a series of payments with his bank details.


How to handle recurring payments?


Recurring payments are handled with the use of the directDebitType of the mandate object which points to the type of usage for the particular order. You should mark orders as First, Recurring and Last according to the table provided above .
